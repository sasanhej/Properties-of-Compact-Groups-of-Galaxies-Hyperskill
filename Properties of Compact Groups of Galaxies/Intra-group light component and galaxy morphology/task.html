Description

The Sérsic index \(n\) and the numerical galaxy type \(T\) characterize galaxy morphology quantitatively. You can estimate the relation between the IGL component and the CG morphology by calculating the correlation of the IGL mean surface brightness with two means: \(n\) and \(T\). For this, we will use the Pearson correlation coefficients.

Objectives

To complete this stage:

  1. Continue working with galaxies_morphology.tsv from the previous stage. Calculate the mean Sérsic index and the mean numerical Hubble stage for each CG. Group data by the Group column. The values of the new table are the mean Sérsic index (n) and the mean numerical galaxy type (T). Create the columns mean_n and mean_T for obtained values with the help of the agg function from pandas.
  2. Use the Group column and merge the data table with the one from groups.tsv you preprocessed in Stage 1;
  3. Plot scatterplots for the following value pairs: mean_mu-mean_n and mean_mu-mean_T;
  4. Conduct the Shapiro-Wilk Normality tests for each variable: mean_mu, mean_n, and mean_T;
  5. Calculate the Pearson correlation coefficients and the corresponding \(p\)-values for testing non-correlation. The null hypothesis of the test is that the considered populations are not correlated;
  6. Print five floating-point numbers separated by one space: \(p\)-values for normality testing of mean_mu, mean_n and mean_T, and \(p\)-values for testing non-correlation for mean_mu-mean_n and mean_mu-mean_T.

After completing, try to answer the question:

  • What is the distribution of the IGL mean surface brightness for galaxies with the different mean Sersic indices and numerical Hubble stages of the groups?
